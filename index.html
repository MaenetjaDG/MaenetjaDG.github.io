About Us


Bolobathaba Media Group was established in 2010 as a film and television production company based in GaModjadji, Limpopo Province. Our core business include film, television, video and sound production, as well as photography. After a few years in business, we realised there was a gap in multimedia skills development and training, and Africa institute of Multimedia AIM® was born. Apart from multimedia production services, the company also offer accredited programmes in journalism, film and television production. We currently implement skills development programmes in partnership with National Electronic Media Institute of South Africa (NEMISA), National Film and Video Foundation (NFVF) and MICT SETA.

Background

Bolobathaba Media Group is owned solely by Molatelo Mainetje-Bossman.

Molatelo is an international award-winning filmmaker and social entrepreneur with 21 years of experience. She is the founder and owner of Bolobathaba; a multimedia production company and the academy Africa Institute of Multimedia AIM®, accredited with MICT SETA and QCTO. Before venturing into business as an independent filmmaker, content creator and philanthropist, Molatelo worked as a freelancer for local and international production companies and has also worked for broadcasters such as SABC, Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC Australia) Africa Bureau and eNCA.

She is the recipient of United Nations Media Peace Award 2007 and SAFTAs 2021 receipt of Outstanding Provincial Contributor Award for her role in training and mentoring young filmmakers and digital content creators in rural South Africa, among other accolades. Her passion for skills development and transfer led to her working with emerging filmmakers as a story consultant and mentor through NFVF and KZN Film Commission. Her debut documentary feature When Babies Don’t Come (2018) won multiple awards internationally.
